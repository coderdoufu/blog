Xinyi Solar - Dec 2020

📬  Receive my latest stock analysis straight to your inbox.

The renewable energy industry is the best investment for the next 10 years.

In this post, I am going to share a few things about Xinyi Solar so that you know why you should invest in this company.

What is Xinyi Solar?#

China is currently the biggest producer of Photovoltaic Glass in the world and Xinyi Solar is recognized as the largest solar glass production base national-wide. Xinyi Solar was spun off group company Xinyi Glass in 2013 and it has multiple production lines in different manufacturing bases around the whole of China, including Tianjin, Beihai Guangxi, and Wuhu Anhui. Additionally, it also set up a base in Melaka, Malaysia. As of today, Xinyi Solar’s global market share has already exceeded 30%.

Why you should invest in Xinyi Solar?#

  • Excellent financial record. Since it was first listed on the main board of the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, its revenue and net income have steadily increased until today with the exception in 2018 due to the impact of China's 531 policy change. COVID-19 has affected its business temporarily for the first half of the year. However, the company was quick enough to roll out different measures including a cost-saving strategy to reduce the impact. Furthermore, Xinyi has also put its focus on developing critical components for dual-glass and bifacial solar panels, which aim to lift its profits further when the demand shifts to these efficient panels.

  • China makes nearly 80% of the world's solar panels and Xinyi is the leader in the industry. Given its current position in the industry, Xinyi has an advantage over its competitors in terms of the cost of material for producing the solar glass. Customers who have already chosen Xinyi will not leave easily as the certification process for changing a new solar glass for the existing photovoltaic modules is tedious and long. This means that it will always benefit from high customers' lifetime value and remain as a leader in the industry.

  • Growing preference for bifacial modules (producing power from both sides as compared to traditional opaque-backsheeted panels) due to increasing penetration rate has created a new demand for solar glassmakers. Since technology has evolved, many solar farm operators are demanding a better and more efficient solar glass to generate more power without increasing the cost. Thanks to the success of the tempering technology for 2mm bifacial solar glass, the cost of bifacial solar glass closely matches with the mono-facial solar glass, but the former can generate 10% more energy and last 30 years as compared to the mono-facial solar glass. This implies a strong demand for solar glass for use in double-glass and bifacial solar modules as customers started to shift to bifacial solar modules.
