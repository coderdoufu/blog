Greetings, investors

Sorry about the delayed portfolio update.

As part of this post, I will provide an overview of my trading activities in August, what are my views regarding the China market conditions at the moment, and how would my current strategies change.

Changes to my portfolio in August#

As I anticipate that the price would retrace to its previous low, which is around $56-$60, I sold part of my Ping An shares at $68.05. Meanwhile, I increased my position in Palantir by selling my EWZ shares.

Palantir Q2 earnings

Is it for a good reason that I'm increasing my position in Palantir?#

Last week, Palantir announced its Q2 earnings and both revenue and earnings beat analyst expectations. Additionally, the company raised its forecast of adjusted free cash flow for the full year 2021 from $150million plus to $300million plus. As a result of this and the announcement that ARK is buying 5.6 million Palantir shares across its actively managed exchange-traded funds, Palantir's share price continued to rise. I have explained previously that Palantir is a unique data analytic company that has won unprecedented government trust. Due to its special relationship with the US government, Palantir is expected to hold the top spot for providing intelligence to the US government for a long time to come, similar to Boeing in the US market. In turn, this also means that allies will continue to depend on Palantir for their intelligence needs. Among the most famous names in Silicon Valley, Palantir has signed deals with IBM, Amazon, and Fujitsu. During Q2 of the company's fiscal year, sales to customers in the U.S. grew by more than 28% year-over-year. Given that it is a fast-growing business with sticky government contracts, I believe its current stock price does not truly reflect its future value (high growth companies typically trade at a premium to their book values).

China market#

People are obviously concerned about China right now. It is the same fear that I had when I had to decide whether or not to sell off the stocks of my Chinese companies during the capitulation phase. Despite my strong optimism regarding China's policy, some westerners may still judge the events through the lens of the communist mindset. I want to go over some of the incidents that took place at my biggest holding company.

Ping An#

Several sources have indicated that Ping An Insurance has been investigated by local regulators due to its investment in property. Ping An confirmed, however, that the regulators have not given any such order to stop selling its alternative investment products. In addition, Ping An Health Care reported a 300% loss increase despite a large inflow of new users. Apparently, the lower first order fee is part of a campaign to attract new customers. Market players worry about the situation after People's Daily called for more scrutiny of the sector, which was worth 54.5 billion yuan (US$8.4 billion) and will reach 63.6 billion yuan this year. China's regulatory crackdown has put a lot of pressure on its share price, just as it has on Chinese tech stocks. Ping An's current share price still seems attractive given that it has nearly retraced back to its 2017 low, though I have been reducing my exposure to it recently.

Alibaba#

Alibaba is one of my favorite companies in China. Besides having an excellent business model, the company is a leading tech company as well as an important driver of innovation in China. As I have previously stated, I may have a bias since I worked there previously. After reviewing the fundamentals of the company, I am more confident that the company is destined for long-term success. Let's take a look at what this company has been experiencing since 2020.

  1. During the Bund Summit in Shanghai, Alibaba Group co-founder and ex-executive chairman, Jack Ma, gave his infamous speech. Ant's mega IPO was thought to have failed due to this speech.
  2. Beijing's antitrust watchdog, the State Administration for Market Regulation, fined Alibaba a record $2.8 billion in April for "er xuan yi" practices, which penalized merchants for selling goods on its platform and on rival marketplaces. Alibaba's fine was equivalent to 4% of its domestic annual sales.
  3. Chinese #MeToo movement is once again in the spotlight following rape allegations at Alibaba
  4. The Chinese government passes one of the world's strictest data privacy laws. It contains provisions that require any organization or individual handling Chinese citizens' personal data to reduce the collection of data and to obtain consent prior to collecting data. The personal data protection laws China passed can be compared with those in Europe's General Data Protection Regulation, which are also closely related.

Beijing's targeting of companies could cause investors trouble. Yet, most of the policies enacted by the Chinese government benefit its citizens, and this has a positive impact on the economy as a whole. In spite of the political motive of President Xi to gain support for a third term at next year's party congress, the crackdown will give startups more room to grow to compete on a much more equal basis with giants. The economy will become more competitive eventually, which will make the country more self-sufficient by driving the country's innovation efforts.

Several people have asked where the best entry point would be for Alibaba since the stock has plunged to a pandemic low. My long-term investors who have followed me have observed that I am accumulating good businesses when the market is hit by a black swarm event, triggering negative sentiment that lowers the price. Due to the growing regulatory risk, the price may not be at the bottom. In reality, many Chinese tech giants have been unloyal to the rules for a long time, but we had never seen such strong sanctions in place before, even after President Xi assumed his position as China's "emperor". As more guidance is put in place, the company's business becomes more transparent, which gradually reduces its risk of being targeted by regulators. They would be more focused on establishing their business on the right track with all these policies.

What my strategy is for Chinese companies#

At this point, I believe that we are close to the capitulation phase where most people are selling their shares, while the remaining investors are not willing to sell at the current price level. As a consequence, the share price would probably remain stable for some time. As a precaution, I would patiently wait for a better market signal (via technical analysis) before buying. So far, I'm still optimistic about the Chinese economy post-pandemic.

My strategy in September#

I will concentrate my attention on crypto in the coming months since I believe that it will reach new highs by the year's end. Since I've had experience in the past, I would control my position to below 5% so that our overall portfolio would not experience too much volatility.

Special notes#

Since I haven't responded to the comments as promptly over the past few weeks, please accept my apology. While I accept the criticism, there are some tips I would like to share with those who follow my trades or have uncopied my portfolio.

  1. You may have noticed that I am one of the few investors who constantly tell copiers not to add money at random times. The habit of topping up my account every month allows me to control my emotions in entering the market. Moreover, this habit would allow me to analyze my own portfolio and make adjustments as necessary to the current market situation, including diversifying my holdings and averaging down the costs of some of the great businesses I invest in.
  2. I am often asked this question: why is my portfolio down? How can you turn it into a profitable one? I always say that no investment can guarantee you a profit. As an example, I have invested in Alibaba since 2020, and its share price has continued to decline. Could you tell me how Alibaba can increase my return? Investing in my portfolio is the same thing. As I have stated repeatedly, I believe in long-term investing and I only invest in companies that are trading at discounts and will grow in the upcoming years. I'd been concerned about short-term market risks for quite a while, but the company's strong fundamentals prompted me to increase my positions. It might be easier for some to leave the portfolio alone and not do anything. As an active investor, I do adjust my positions as the market conditions change. Also, my decision not to do anything to my portfolio is based on my belief that the short-term fluctuations would only affect the paper money and not the actual money that I will be able to realize in the future.

I hope this month's update will give you some insight into how I view the market.
