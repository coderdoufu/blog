June/2021

📬  Receive my latest stock analysis straight to your inbox.

Dear investors,

I have completed my monthly deposit yesterday.

We have finally entered the second half of the year. In today's post, I will discuss what I have been doing in June, my views on the market in H2, and explain why I have fallen short in H1.

𝐖𝐡𝐚𝐭 𝐡𝐚𝐯𝐞 𝐈 𝐛𝐞𝐞𝐧 𝐝𝐨𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐢𝐧 𝐉𝐮𝐧𝐞?#

A little over 1% of my portfolio fell in June. When compared with other portfolio managers, this is pretty disappointing. When compared to portfolios that have a higher weightage on crypto, our portfolio's performance is still comparable. In June, why did I take a relatively large position in crypto? This is primarily because of short-term inflation hedges. There have been a lot of indicators (including CPI) pointing to inflation surges in recent months (tapering) from the Fed. I expect the money to flow into crypto assets or commodities. Further on-chain analysis has also shown that many leveraged positions have been washed out during the market capitulation, meaning that crypto traders are now holding as opposed to trading. Despite my expectations for more downward movement, I think that a short-term upward movement is more likely to occur due to inflation risks. The change in China's policy has completely changed the rule, and I have been hit unexpectedly by my short-term trading stop loss.

Our stock portfolios are doing well apart from the crypto market. $PLTR (Palantir Technologies Inc.) , Airbus, Micron, and Tui contributed to our short-term profits. My decision to sell out all my $MU (Micron Technology, Inc.) positions was based on my belief that the supply-over-demand balance would likely peak in H2. This is a vicious cycle that I have been trapped into before, and I want to avoid it this time. Even so, the share price may not fall. Its DRAM and NAND prices might still rise due to the strong end-market demand. In terms of PLTR, it is still overpriced (based on traditional valuation methods). Its technology advantage, however, provides much greater room for growth. In the upcoming quarters, its recent collaborations with several top industry players will contribute to the company's reputation in the long term and bring in more profits. This would potentially lead to a huge breakout for its current share price.

𝐇𝐞𝐫𝐞 𝐚𝐫𝐞 𝐦𝐲 𝐭𝐡𝐨𝐮𝐠𝐡𝐭𝐬 𝐨𝐧 𝐭𝐡𝐞 𝐦𝐚𝐫𝐤𝐞𝐭 𝐢𝐧 𝐇𝟐.#

$SPX500 has gone insanely high in value. Still, there are a number of undervalued stocks. Stocks of consumer and financial companies are still reasonably priced compared to technology companies, for example. I'll keep buying these stocks even if their shares go down in price. As long-term investments, most of the stocks in my portfolio would not be sold in H2. In light of the fact that inflation remains a concern in H2, I would not expect these stocks to fluctuate as much as overvalued technology stocks.

In the Chinese market, the Hang Seng Index has reached an undervalued level. Despite the fact that China's antitrust policy may have scared away many foreign investors, this should be viewed as a positive trend that would do more good than harm. Keeping their businesses competitive with the US can certainly be accomplished with the antitrust policy. The fact that many foreign investors have lower allocations to China market is certainly an opportunity for us to acquire more undervalued stocks so that they can generate long-term profits for our portfolio. My focus in H2 will be Ping An Insurance and Alibaba.

𝐖𝐡𝐚𝐭 𝐰𝐞𝐧𝐭 𝐰𝐫𝐨𝐧𝐠 𝐢𝐧 𝐇𝟏?#

Throughout H1, I have mainly focused on short-term investment and trading (as I explained at the beginning of the year). The strategies, however, seem not to be working and caused my portfolio's performance to fall short of my expectations in H1. When the bull market is nearing its end, I made the mistake of investing too much in the Crypto market. The fund allocation of more than 5% into such a volatile market should have been more carefully chosen. Another mistake that I made was that I invested too much in small-cap companies. In spite of their low valuation, they are too volatile, since their current stock price is based mostly on speculation and they are unlikely to reach their fundamental valuation in the near future.

Knowing that I've made mistakes, I've decided to alter my strategy in H2. The next step is to focus more on valuation and find the stocks that are mispriced in the market. In addition, I will steer clear of highly volatile assets and accumulate undervalued stocks for long-term investment.

In closing, I want to emphasize once again that not all investors can stay positive or beat the market all the time. Even so, I still believe that our investment profits will be substantial in the long run, if we are able to identify our mistakes and quickly adapt to the market.

Thank you.
