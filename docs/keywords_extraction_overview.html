Keywords Extraction

Keywords extraction is an important subtask of Natural Language Processing. Given a length article, keywords provide a convenient way to understand the essence of the article without spending your precious time reading through the entire article. For machine learning model, keywords serve as important tools to reduce the noice effect for summarization and feature extraction. They are also used in many other downstream tasks like article classification, search engine, knowledge graph construction, topic clustering, recommendation system and many more.

Keywords extraction can be done in unsupervised and supervised setting. Let's talk about it in more details.

Unsupervised Keywords Extraction#

This is a popular approach for many industry application as it doesn't require a lot of human labels. This is important for some domains such as news publisher. The amount of news produced each day might have different topics and most of the topics are seasonal. Therefore, the keywords might be very different for every articles. The company might waste a lot of money to invest in human resources for labelling work if they choose a supervised approach. So, unsupervised keywords extraction should be a better option in this case.

The general process of unsupervised keywords extractions is as follow:

  1. extract candidate words
  2. score and rank for each candidate
  3. select the topK candidates as keywords

There are basically three popular methods for unsupervised keywords extractions:

  1. Term frequency (TF) and Term frequency–Inverse document frequency (TFIDF)
  2. PageRank and TextRank
  3. Linear discriminant analysis (LDA)

TFIDF#

TFIDF is a very popular method for keyword extraction. The main idea about TFIDF is that it is used to calculate the important of a word to a document. There are two separate calculation needed to be done for TFIDF.

  1. Term frequency - calculate the count of a word in a document. The more frequent that a particular word appears in a document, the more important it is to the document.
  2. Inverse document frequency - calculate the number of document which contains the word. The main idea is if the word is important to a particular document, it should only appear in the document itself, but not other. For example, we might have seen words such as 'you', 'the', 'a' appearing in most of the document. This means that they are common words but not important keywords that could be use to represent a particular document.

Example#

Given a sentence "The olympic champion just won its title. In the history of the Olympics, this is the first time an individual has won the same title twice.".
