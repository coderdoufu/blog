Recsys Overview

What is Content Recommendation?#

If you visit a website, have you ever seen a section called "You may also like..."? For example, today is a holiday on which I plan to make cupcakes for my girlfriend. As a result, I decided to buy a cupcake topper on Amazon to decorate my cupcake. If you scroll to the bottom of this website, you will see a list of items under the heading "Customers who bought this item also bought". Using AI algorithms, Amazon generates this list of relevant items that you are likely to buy alongside the cupcake topper. An algorithm like this falls under the category of "content recommendation".

Why do we need content recommendation?#

Information is explosive in today's world. Take the BBC website as an example. There are a number of articles that you might not find suitable. Consider that you are passionate about sports and often visit sports-related articles. Further reading of sports articles can be accomplished in several different ways. To do this, you can leave the website and search on Google. Another option is to search the relevant articles on the same website. While both methods require you to search for relevant articles, neither guarantees you will be able to locate an article you want to read. How can the system help you by recommending relevant articles so that you don't have to search for them? Content recommendation is the answer. Based on the time and device that you are using right now, an algorithm can recommend content that you would be interested in reading. For instance, if you use 4G instead of a wi-fi connection on your smartphone, the algorithm is unlikely to recommend video content and will likely recommend shorter and more relevant articles instead. You might be an avid sports fan, for example, and you enjoy reading articles about Cristiano Ronaldo. Thus, the algorithm can map your interest precisely and recommend articles related to football.

Businesses can benefit from content recommendations by increasing sales and developing new products. Your customer will be more likely to become a loyal member if your system is able to accurately capture their interest.

How does the system know so well about me?#

Let's return to the Amazon shopping example. Now you might be asking yourself: How does the system know that I'm going to need the item on the list? In order to better understand your needs and wants as a consumer, businesses use a number of techniques. You may find another user on the system with the same profile (for example, gender, age, clicked items, frequent visit hours, etc...) and he just happened to have bought the cupcake topper some time ago. The next thing he bought might be something you'd be interested in. Therefore, the system recommends the item to you in hopes that you will purchase it along with the cupcake topper.

In most recommendations systems, hidden interests are also considered. It is possible that there will be recommendations that don't align with your shopping habits. There is a purpose behind this. Using the 'surprise' item, the algorithm wants to explore your other interests.

Is there any difference between search engine and recommendation engine?#

In search engines, users have specific objectives, so their search scopes are more focused. Imagine that user A searches for Lee Chong Wei (a legendary badminton player in Malaysia) and we know for sure that he/she is interested in badminton, so the system can return all the information about Lee Chong Wei. We cannot recommend him/her more than 5 articles since there are only five slots available for recommendations at the end of the article. Imagine that we have a recommendation section at the end of the article and need to suggest the next articles that might spark his/her interest. It is important to think carefully about his behavior here and rank the retrieved articles (after applying some algorithms and selecting a number of candidate articles) so that he/she is shown only the top 5 most confident articles.

Types of recommendation#

Recommendations can be non-personalized or personalized.

Items that are not personalized are often recommended to a large number of site visitors. We sometimes see sections such as most popular articles or trending articles on a page. The articles are simply collected based on the statistics of website users. These recommended articles can also be customized to certain groups of users. It is likely that people in Singapore will notice trending articles that happened there, and the same logic applies to people living in other regions.

For personalized recommendations, every user will see articles that are relevant to their behavior, and these recommended articles are different for each user.

The Design of Recommendation System?#

Let's talk about how to build a recommendation system.

The overall system is divided into online and offline components. Whenever we use an offline system, the data must first be collected. You can use either your user data or the product metadata. As an example, let's look at Amazon. On the product page you can see the price, description, title, brand, and owner. You will also see ratings, product categories, and other technical details about the products. As we build a profile for the product in our recommendation system, we find this information extremely valuable. Next, we will create the model. Detailed information about these models will be covered in separate articles. Your model will begin collecting feedback as soon as it starts serving customers. Generally, this is done through AB testing, where multiple deployed models are compared at the same time, and whichever generates the highest Click Through Rate (CTR) (the criteria will vary according to the business scenario) is considered the winner. After that, you will improve/retrain your model based on newer data (this varies depending on business needs and different use cases).

Ultimately, you want to serve your customers with your system. So, for online systems, you need to consider the entire process from API requests to responses back to the client.

Our next topic will be the offline system since the majority of what happens for recommendation systems happens offline.
