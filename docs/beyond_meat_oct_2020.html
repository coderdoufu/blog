Beyond Meat - Oct 2020

Sold Out

[ Jan 2021 Updated ] I have dumped all my Beyond Meat shares.

In this post, I am going to summarize the achievement of Beyond Meat so far, why I am in a long position for this stock, and what the risks involved in this stock.

Meat is not King#

Launched in 2009, the company's mission is to change the way of how humans absorb protein traditionally. Through lab-grown meat, the company hopes to fight for its market share in the global meat market, which accounts for 1.8T as recorded in 2018. Given its leader position in the booming plant-based protein movement, it has so far scored an impressive number of partnerships with giant food chains in China and the US. Today, Beyond's products are available at approximately 112,000 retail and foodservice outlets in 85 countries worldwide.

Key partnerships#

  • In August 2019, Yum Brands tested the companyâ€™s plant-based chicken nuggets at an Atlanta KFC restaurant. The result was astonishing, the product was sold out within 5 hours. This strong consumer demand proved its value and persuaded KFC to add new Beyond Fried Chicken into its menu in almost 100 stores in Charlotte.
  • In April 2020, the company achieved its first foray into China consumer market through collaboration with Starbucks (Starbucks has almost 3300 stores in China);
  • Subsequently, Yum Chinaâ€™s KFC, Taco Bell, and Pizza Hut also announced that they will sell its product in many cities in China;
  • At the end of June 2020, Alibaba announced that it will start selling meatless burger patties at 50 Freshippo (name of the supermarket) locations in Shanghai.
  • Walmart triples the availability of the Beyond Burger from approximately 800 locations to more than 2,400 stores nationwide in early October.

Why it will continue growing?#

Many reasons are causing Beyond Meat to continue growing, these are the reasons why I think its growth rate would be sustainable:

  • Exponential growth in terms of revenue. Its revenue has continued to increase since 2016 and in 2019 alone it has recorded about $298 million revenue, which accounts for almost 200% up from its previous fiscal year.
  • The demand for plant-based protein in China has constantly increased over the years. As the company begins to venture into the Chinese market, it could push its revenue growth even higher. On top of that, the demand for meatless products grows even higher when African Swine Flu hits domestic pork production. Now, China has discovered the new swine flu that has the potential to cause a new wave of a pandemic to the world. This could somehow affect people's lifestyles to adopt a healthier choice of protein.
  • Beyond Meat is going to open up its first factory outside of the US, which is a Zandbergen facility in Zoeterwoude, the Netherlands. This means that the demand in Europe and the Middle East is growing and a new co-manufacturing facility in Europe could further reduce its cost of delivery. This also means that Beyond Meat is ready to expand its products into a broader market.

What are the risk involved?#

Letâ€™s now look at the risk we need to take if we decide to invest in this stock

  • The demand could be caused by temporary customer curiosity. As these meatless products did not exist before, consumers would be more willing to try them. However, whether such a trend could be sustainable, we will have to wait for a more confirming signal in the coming quarters.
  • Fierce competition in the meatless industry. Beyond Meat isnâ€™t the only company selling meatless product in the market right now, there are other renowned companies like Impossible Foods offering the same quality products. Furthermore, plant-based start-ups such as Starfield and ZhenMeat continued to expand their footprints across China cities. This creates extra pressure for Beyond Meat to dominant in the plant-based food industry.
  • Beyond Meat is a premium high growth stock. The company is currently still unprofitable and its free cash flow is decreasing every year. Comparing to its industry peers such as Tyson Foods and Hormel Foods, its forward PE ratio is more than 10 times higher than its peers.
  • Beyond was downgraded by JP Morgan due to aggressive competition from Impossible Foods.
